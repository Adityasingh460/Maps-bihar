Quick Facts

  • Area: 94,163 km² (Ranked 12th in India)
  • Population: Over 120 million
  • Districts: 38 districts
  • Divisions: 9 administrative divisions
  • Major Rivers: Ganges, Sone, Gandak, Kosi

Geographical Features

Bihar has three distinct regions: the Himalayan foothills in the north, the fertile Gangetic plain, and the Chota Nagpur plateau in the south. The state has a humid subtropical climate with hot summers and cool winters.

Historical & Cultural Landmarks

UNESCO World Heritage Sites

  • Mahabodhi Temple - The place where Buddha attained enlightenment
  • Nalanda Mahavihara - Ruins of the ancient university complex
  • Vikramashila Mahavihara - Important ancient Buddhist university

Other Important Sites

  • Patna Museum - Houses artifacts from the Mauryan and Gupta periods
  • Barabar Caves - The oldest surviving rock-cut caves in India
  • Sasaram - Tomb of Sher Shah Suri, an architectural masterpiece
